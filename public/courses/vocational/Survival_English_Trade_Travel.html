Target: Zero/Basic English (A1/A2 Survival). Total Duration: 16 Hours.

Module: Survival English for Travel (Weeks 1-4)

Session (Time) Key Theme & Objectives Core Content Focus Trainer Notes
1 (2h) Alphabet, Numbers, and Time. Phonetics, Numbers 1-100, "What time is it?" Use the phonetic alphabet aggressively. Focus on listening/repetition.

7 (2h) Shopping & Currency. HOW MUCH IS, Cash/Card, Too expensive. Integrate number recognition and currency names.
8 (2h) Emergencies & Review. Help, Doctor, Police, I am sick/lost. Full Travel Simulation. Stress the importance of volume and clarity in an emergency.

3. Core Content: Time and Dates (30 min)

Focus: Asking and stating the time and dates (crucial for flights/meetings).

  • **Asking Time:** "What time is it?" / "When is the meeting?"
  • **Stating Time:** "It is five o'clock." / "It is 3:30." (Use of **IT IS**.)

4. Core Activity: Drill: Asking and Answering (45 min)

Goal: Fluently state and repeat numbers/times without hesitation.

Activity 1: Phone Code Practice: Trainee A reads a phone number slowly in English. Trainee B reads it back to confirm.

Activity 2: Check-in Time Drill: Learners shout out the time from presented clocks/written times.

Session 2: Airport & Travel Terminology (2 Hours)

1. Warm-up Session: Travel Sign Matching (15 min)

Goal: Visually identify key travel nouns (Passport Control, Gate, Baggage Claim).

Activity: Trainer shows images of airport signs. Learners name the English word.

2. Core Content: Key Airport Vocabulary (30 min)

Focus: Purely functional travel nouns.

3. Core Content: Grammar Focus: Imperatives (30 min)

Focus: Understanding and using simple commands and polite requests.

  • **Giving Instructions:** "**Go** here." / "**Wait** there." / "**Show** me your passport." (Base form of the verb.)
  • **Polite Request:** "**Please** wait here." (Adding **PLEASE**.)
  • **Prohibition:** "**Do not** enter." / "**No smoking**."

4. Core Activity: Role-Play: Check-in Dialogue (45 min)

Goal: Practice responding to staff commands and asking simple questions.

Scenario: Check-in desk. Staff: "Passport and ticket, please." Traveler: "Where is Gate A2?"

Session 3: On the Plane & Immigration (2 Hours)

1. Warm-up Session: "Yes/No" Question Drill (15 min)

Goal: Practice rapid comprehension of simple questions (crucial for immigration). Learners respond only with **"Yes"** or **"No"**.

Example: "Are you traveling alone?" / "Is your visit for two weeks?"

2. Core Content: Immigration & In-Flight Vocabulary (30 min)

Focus: Terms needed for official interviews and in-flight service.

3. Core Content: Grammar Focus: Modals (30 min)

Focus: Using **CAN** for requests and Simple Present for answering official questions.

  • **Immigration Answer:** "**I am** here for business." (Simple Present Tense.)
  • **In-Flight Request:** "**Can I have** water, please?" (Modal **CAN**.)
  • **Understanding Question:** "**Do you have** anything to declare?" (Focus on recognizing **DO YOU**.)

4. Core Activity: Role-Play: Immigration Interview (45 min)

Goal: Successfully navigate a short, focused official interview (5 questions).

Challenge: Officer asks the traveler to spell their name, forcing use of the phonetic alphabet.

1. Warm-up Session: Map Reading Drill (15 min)

3. Core Content: Grammar Focus: Location and Price (30 min)

Focus: Mastering the essential questions: **WHERE IS** and **HOW MUCH IS IT**.

  • **Asking Location:** "**Where is** the taxi stand?"
  • **Asking Price:** "**How much is** the ticket?"
  • **Directions:** "**Go straight**." / "**Turn left**." / "**It is** next to the park." (Imperatives and Prepositions.)

4. Core Activity: Role-Play: Taking a Taxi (45 min)

Goal: Successfully negotiate the price and destination of a taxi ride.

Setup: Traveler must ask for the taxi, state the destination, and ask for the price *before* getting in.

Challenge: Driver quotes a large price. Traveler must repeat the number to confirm.

3. Core Content: Grammar Focus: Needs and Clarification (30 min)

Focus: Using **I NEED** and Simple Future Tense for policy.

  • **Stating Need:** "**I need** one towel." / "**I need** the Wi-Fi password."

4. Core Activity: Role-Play: Check-in/Check-out (45 min)

Goal: Conduct a complete check-in and a basic check-out.

Setup: Traveler must confirm the booking, ask for two services (Wi-Fi, extra pillow), and ask for the final price at check-out.
